Expert Guidance: Critical Next Steps

This section provides the *absolute must-do* actions for each competition type based on the current phase. Follow these steps immediately after the initial team meeting.

Design & Build Focus (IAM3D, SDC, XRC)

As a Project Manager, your teams must prioritize **proof-of-concept** over complex final design right now. Time is your biggest enemy.

  • Step 1: **System Breakdown & Role Lock.** Immediately break the competition problem into 3-4 core mechanical subsystems (e.g., drive, actuation, sensor). Assign a **subsystem owner** for each; no shared responsibility for core components.
  • Step 2: **Minimum Viable Prototype (MVP) CAD.** Design the *simplest* version of the mechanism that theoretically completes the mission. Focus on function verification, not aesthetics. This should be completed within the first 3 weeks.
  • Step 3: **Treasurer/Advisor Gateway.** Before ordering *any* materials or beginning *any* fabrication, the team lead **must** have their MVP CAD approved by the Faculty Advisor and their final parts budget approved by the Treasurer. This prevents costly reworks.
  • Step 4: **Weekly Testing Ritual.** Start a culture where the final hour of every team meeting is dedicated to **testing** something—whether it's motor response, code stability, or material strength. Test early, test often.

Presentation Focus (Old Guard, Elevator Pitch)

As a Communications Coach, your goal is a killer opening and a tight, clear narrative. Practice is the only variable you control.

  • **Elevator Pitch (Under 60 Seconds):**
  • **Opening Hook:** Start with a shocking statistic or a powerful, short story about the problem. E.g., *"Engineers waste 40% of their project time due to unnecessary friction—our design eliminates 90% of it."*
  • **Core Theme:** Define *one* clear message (e.g., "Efficiency through Simplicity"). Every word must support this theme.
  • **Old Guard (15 Minute Presentation):**
  • **Opening Hook:** Use the first 60 seconds to clearly state the **Problem, Solution, and Personal Contribution** to the project. E.g., *"My capstone project solved the problem of [X] using [Y] methodology, and my unique contribution was optimizing [Z] parameter."*
  • **Core Theme:** Structure the narrative around the engineering method: **Need > Design > Analysis > Results**. Avoid getting stuck in the background and move quickly to your technical solution.
